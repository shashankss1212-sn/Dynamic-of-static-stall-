2D Flat Plate Flow Transition Analysis: From Laminar to Chaotic Regimes at Low Reynolds Numbers

This repository contains a numerical study of flow transition over a two-dimensional flat plate at low Reynolds numbers, investigating the progression from steady laminar flow to chaotic behaviour across varying angles of attack (AOA). The simulation uses an air velocity of 0.148 m/s and examines how changes in the angle of attack (α) influence flow dynamics, vortex formation, and the onset of stall.

Overview

Understanding the transition from coherent to turbulent flow is critical in aerodynamic design, particularly at low Reynolds number regimes where behaviour is highly sensitive to geometric and operational parameters. This study analyses complex vortex dynamics at both the leading and trailing edges as the angle of attack is systematically varied.

Methodology

Three advanced time-series analysis techniques are employed to characterise vortex energy and flow behaviour: Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) for frequency domain analysis, Continuous Wavelet Transform (CWT) for time-frequency localisation, and Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD) for extracting intrinsic mode functions from non-stationary signals. Recurrence Quantification Analysis (RQA) is further applied to distinguish between periodic, quasi-periodic, and chaotic flow regimes.

Research Goals

Map the stalling phenomenon across varying angles of attack

Identify transitions from coherent laminar structures to turbulent chaotic states

Quantify vortex energy distribution at the leading and trailing edges

Apply dynamical systems theory to aerodynamic flow characterisation

Applications

Findings are relevant to micro air vehicle (MAV) design, wind energy systems, and any engineering application operating in low Reynolds number environments where accurate prediction of flow transition and stall is essential.
